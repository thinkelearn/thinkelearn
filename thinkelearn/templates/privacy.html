Children's Privacy

THINK eLearn Inc. is committed to protecting the privacy of children who use our educational services. This section explains how we handle personal information from children under the age of 13 in compliance with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA).

Our Privacy-First Approach

We have adopted a simple, privacy-first design that protects children while providing quality educational services:

  • We don't ask for dates of birth - We never collect or store age information from any users
  • We don't try to determine who is a child - We avoid having "actual knowledge" of user ages
  • OAuth-only for everyone - All users must authenticate via Google or Microsoft
  • Minimal data collection - We collect only what's necessary for educational purposes (name, email, learning progress)
  • No age-based restrictions - All users are treated equally with access to the same features

How Children Can Access Our Platform

Children under 13 can access our platform using parent-managed accounts through COPPA-compliant family account systems:

  • Google Family Link: Parent-managed Google accounts for children under 13. Parents create the account, review Google's privacy policies, provide verifiable consent, and manage access through the Family Link dashboard.
  • Microsoft Family Safety: Parent-managed Microsoft accounts for children under 13. Parents create the account, review Microsoft's privacy policies, provide verifiable consent, and manage access through the Microsoft Family Safety dashboard.

Key Point: We rely on Google and Microsoft to handle age verification and parental consent. Parents have already provided consent when they created the family account. We do not collect additional consent or age information ourselves.

How This Protects Children

When a child under 13 accesses our platform using Google Family Link or Microsoft Family Safety:

  1. The parent has already created and verified the child's account with Google or Microsoft
  2. The parent has reviewed and consented to Google's or Microsoft's privacy practices for children
  3. The parent manages the child's account through the Family Link or Microsoft Family Safety dashboard
  4. When the child signs into our platform using their family account, we receive only basic information (name, email, account ID) that the parent has already consented to share
  5. Parents can review, manage, and revoke access to our platform through their family account settings

By relying on these established, COPPA-compliant identity providers, we ensure that proper parental consent and verification has been obtained before children access our educational services.

Why This Approach Works

Our OAuth-only approach provides superior privacy protection for all users, including children:

  • We don't need to know ages: COPPA requires appropriate handling when we have "actual knowledge" of collecting from children. By not collecting dates of birth, we avoid having actual knowledge while ensuring children can only access through COPPA-compliant systems.
  • Leverages industry leaders: Google and Microsoft have invested heavily in COPPA-compliant family account systems with verified parental consent, regular audits, and robust parent controls.
  • Parents already trust these platforms: Families use familiar tools (Family Link, Microsoft Family Safety) they already understand and trust.
  • Better security for everyone: No passwords to breach, support for two-factor authentication, and professional security teams protecting accounts.
  • Simpler for us, safer for children: We avoid being the system that determines ages, verifies consent, and tracks birthdays - reducing complexity and legal risk.
  • No age transitions: Children use the same account their whole lives - no awkward "turning 13" logic or feature unlocking.

Your Privacy Rights

Under Canadian privacy law (PIPEDA - Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right to Correction

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. You can update most of your information directly through your account settings.

Right to Deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data, subject to certain legal exceptions (such as financial records required for tax compliance). You can delete your account through your account settings or by contacting us.

Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit that data to another service provider.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we rely on your consent to process personal data, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. This includes opting out of marketing emails or SMS notifications.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe we have not complied with Canadian privacy laws, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada at www.priv.gc.ca.

Cookie Policy

A cookie is a small file, stored on a user's device by a website. Its purpose is to collect data relating to the user's browsing habits and to maintain session information. You can choose to be notified each time a cookie is transmitted. You can also choose to disable cookies entirely in your internet browser, but this may prevent you from using certain features of our Site, including logging in and accessing courses.

We use the following types of cookies on our Site:

1. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of our Site and cannot be disabled. They include:

  • Session Cookies: Used to keep you logged in as you navigate between pages. These cookies are deleted when you close your browser.
  • Authentication Cookies: Used to verify your identity when you log in with email/password or Google OAuth.
  • CSRF Protection Cookies: Used to prevent cross-site request forgery attacks and protect your account security.
  • Course Progress Cookies: Used to track your position within SCORM course content during a learning session.

2. Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance the functionality of our Site by remembering your preferences:

  • Language and region preferences
  • Display preferences and accessibility settings
  • Course filter selections
  • Dashboard view preferences

3. Analytical Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Site so we can improve user experience:

  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Navigation paths and user flows
  • Device and browser information
  • Error and performance monitoring

Analytical data is anonymized and aggregated. We do not use this data to identify individual users.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are created by services other than our Site. We use the following third-party cookies:

  • Google OAuth: When you log in with Google, Google may set cookies to maintain your authentication session. These cookies are governed by Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Stripe: When processing payments, Stripe may set cookies for fraud detection and secure payment processing. These cookies are governed by Stripe's Privacy Policy.

Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Please note: Disabling strictly necessary cookies will prevent you from logging in and accessing course content.
